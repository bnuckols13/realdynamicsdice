Facilitator Guidelines

Core Principles for Facilitation:

  • Start each session at levels appropriate to group cohesion and safety
  • Allow silence after promptsâ€”let responses emerge organically
  • Model vulnerability and authentic response
  • Track when defenses activate and name them compassionately
  • Celebrate small risks and moments of genuine contact

Reading Group Readiness

Begin with Levels 1-3 when the group is new or energy feels tentative

Move to Levels 4-7 once you notice: consistent attendance, members referring to each other outside sessions, authentic laughter and tears

Explore Levels 8-10 when: members spontaneously share vulnerable content, the group can hold heavy emotions, there's trust in the container

Venture to Levels 11-13 sparingly and with intentionâ€”these often emerge naturally in moments of crisis, breakthrough, or deep established trust

Supporting Authentic Responses

When someone shares: Resist the urge to fix, teach, or redirect. Your role is to witness and reflect.

When there's silence: Let it breathe. Count to 30 before offering another prompt.

When defenses appear: Name them gently: "I notice we moved away from that feelingâ€”would anyone like to say what just happened?"

When depth emerges: Slow down. Make eye contact. Let the group feel the significance together.
